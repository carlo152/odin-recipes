Quesadilla Mexicana

Description

Quesadilla Mexicana is a simple yet satisfying dish featuring a tortilla filled with melted cheese and often

accompanied by meats, vegetables, or beans. Traditionally made with a corn tortilla, quesadillas can also

be prepared with flour tortillas for a softer texture.

They’re quick to prepare, making them a perfect breakfast, lunch, or snack. Served with salsa, guacamole,

or sour cream, quesadillas are a beloved Mexican staple that can be customized endlessly to suit any

taste.

Ingredients

  • 4 large flour or corn tortillas
  • 2 cups shredded cheese (Oaxaca, mozzarella, or cheddar)
  • 1 cup cooked chicken, beef, or sautéed vegetables (optional)
  • 2 tbsp butter or vegetable oil
  • Salsa, guacamole, and sour cream for serving

Steps

  1. Heat a skillet over medium heat and lightly grease with butter or oil.
  2. Place one tortilla in the skillet and sprinkle half of the cheese evenly over it.
  3. Add optional fillings such as chicken, beef, or vegetables.
  4. Place another tortilla on top (or fold in half if using a single tortilla).
  5. Cook for 2–3 minutes per side until the tortilla is golden and the cheese is melted.
  6. Remove from heat and cut into wedges.
  7. Serve hot with salsa, guacamole, and sour cream.
